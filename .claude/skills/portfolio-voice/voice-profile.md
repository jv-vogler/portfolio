# Voice profile — João's portfolio blog

**Rhythm.** Wide mix. Short punchy lines ("Yup.", "It's not rocket science.") drop between longer clause-heavy setups. Fragments and one-word paragraphs are fine.

**Register.** Casual-technical. Precision sits next to "POOF!", "voilà", "phew!". Silly about serious material; never sloppy with the material itself. Second person ("you") is the default — the reader is always in the room.

**Openings never start with the topic.** An analogy, a scene, or a hook lands first; the connection to the tech comes a paragraph later. Verbatim:

- "Memento mori. There are only two guaranteed things in life..."
- "Kids, I'm gonna tell you an incredible story. The story of how I met your website."
- "Just like the Moon is constantly influencing planet Earth's ocean tides..."
- "Imagine you just got paid and can finally buy the laptop..."

**Closings tie back to the opening image**, state the tradeoff (never a verdict), and often swing for a callback joke: "If you need anything, your wish is my command." / "Mastering that judgment is what separates good design from just memorizing diagrams."

**Punctuation habits.** Em-dashes present but never clustered (3+ in one paragraph = AI tell). Parentheticals frequent for asides. Strikethrough for self-correcting jokes ("Uncle Ben ~~Parker~~ Linus"). Ellipses for dramatic pauses. ALL CAPS on single words only ("WHAAAT?"). Exclamation points exist, one per paragraph max.

**Rhetorical moves.** Fake reader Q&A ("Okay, but how does..." / "I'm glad you asked!"). Pop-culture drops as analogies (LOTR, Star Wars, Spiderman). Thematic/punny headings, not dry-technical ones ("Grave Mistakes", "Money pages", "Forms under the hood"). Every post lands on "not a silver bullet — here's what it depends on".

**Anti-patterns — not used.** "Let's explore" / "In this post we'll" / "dive in" / "deep dive". AI-isms: "delve", "leverage" (as verb), "seamless", "robust", "cutting-edge". Em-dash clouds. Corporate hedging without naming what it depends on. Apologetic openings. Marketing outros. Fake enthusiasm — dry understatement lands harder.

**Calibration sentences (verbatim):**

1. "Yup. And by the way, it includes the documentation."
2. "If someone ever told you that disk size doesn't matter, I'm sorry to be the one giving you the bad news… but you've been lied to."
3. "There's something elegant and maybe even poetic about capturing moments in code just as we do in life."
4. "Nobody likes to hear just what they have done wrong. Give users a cookie when they behave and fill in the inputs correctly."
5. "Like any design pattern, the Memento Pattern isn't a silver bullet; it's all about trade-offs."

**Dev log sanity floor.** Posts under `content/blog/devlog-*` also answer to `docs/project-ascension/devlog.md §3.3`: first-person singular, past tense for shipped work, no apologies, one joke max, no em-dash-heavy AI prose. A floor for dev logs, not a ceiling for other posts.
